Osso Buco

Why this recipe :

Osso Buco is a real traditinal italian dish. Its take time but if you want to feed people you love it's the perfect dish

This is a great recipe for anyone who wants to learn to cook.

Mastering Osso Bucco means mastering cutting vegetables in mirepoix, deglazing with white wine and simmering a stew.

For 4 people, you'll need :

  • 4
    Veal shanks
  • 1
    Yellow onion
  • 1
    Carrot
  • a stalk of
    Celery
  • 15 cL
    White wine
  • 20 cL
    Meat broth
  • 60 g
    Butter
  • 4 Tbsp
    Olive oil
  • 40 g
    Flour
  • a clove
    Garlic
  • a bunch
    Parsley
  • 1
    Organic Lemon
  • some
    Salt&Pepper

Instructions :

  1. With the onions, celery and carrots, make a mirepoix.

  2. Cut the edges of the stanks at 3 places for them to stay flat when cooked.

  3. Sear the mirepoix in a pot, in half of the oil and a third of the butter, and cook it at very low heat during 10 minutes. Reserve.

  4. Pour the rest of the oil and an other third of butter and brown the meat 5 minutes. Add the mirepoix.

  5. Deglaze with the white wine. Let the wine evaporate 5 minutes. Salt and pepper, then, add half of a glass of the broth.

  6. Cover and cook for 1 h 20 at very low heat.

  7. Turn the meat from time to time and add more broth as the sauce thickens.

  8. Prepare the gremolata : finely chop the pressed garlic, parsley and grated zest of half a lemon with a knife.

  9. The meat is cooked when it comes off the bone. Mix the sauce that remains in the pot with the rest of the butter and half of the gremolata.

  10. Return the pieces of osso-buco to the casserole dish. Leave on the heat for 2 minutes then serve on a plate, cover with sauce and sprinkle with the rest of the gremolata. Serve with a risotto.
